AI data centers trigger a third U.S. federal grid emergency in six months
techtimes.com
July 4, 2026
  • On June 30, Energy Secretary Chris Wright signed two emergency orders under Section 202(c) of the Federal Power Act — a 1935 wartime statute — directing the PJM regional grid to keep power flowing to AI data centers, the third such intervention in six months.
  • Reporting warns the pattern is running plants (including diesel backup) harder, and could add roughly $70/month to household electricity bills by 2028 while degrading local air quality.
  • For executives, it crystallizes power availability as the binding constraint on AI expansion — and an emerging political, cost, and ESG liability that will shape where and how fast capacity comes online.
Alibaba's DAMO Academy agent "Elements Claw" discovers four new superconductors, validated in the lab
pandaily.com
July 4, 2026
  • Alibaba DAMO Academy, with Renmin University and the University of Chinese Academy of Sciences, unveiled Elements Claw — described as the first AI agent purpose-built for superconductor discovery.
  • Powered by a 1B-parameter model trained on 125M molecular and crystal structures, it screened 2.4M stable crystal structures in ~28 GPU-hours, surfaced ~68,000 candidates, and produced four previously unknown superconductors confirmed experimentally.
  • Unlike prior structure-prediction efforts (DeepMind's GNoME, Microsoft's MatterGen), it runs an end-to-end workflow — literature review, synthesis feasibility, toxicity and cost checks — and published prediction data for all 2.4M materials.
  • For context, the SuperCon database has catalogued only ~2,000 superconductors over a century.

Poolside open-weights Laguna XS 2.1, a 33B coding model that runs on a single GPU
techtimes.com
July 4, 2026
  • San Francisco lab Poolside released (July 2) its first open-weight model, Laguna XS 2.1 — a 33B-parameter mixture-of-experts coding agent that activates only ~3B parameters per token and fits on a single consumer GPU (~16–20 GB VRAM at INT4).
  • It posts a 5.4-point gain over its predecessor on SWE-bench Multilingual (to 63.1%) and ships with companion "DFlash" speculative-decoding models that roughly double local inference speed.
  • It is distributed under the new OpenMDW-1.1 weights license, which Poolside argues gives enterprise legal teams cleaner footing than Apache 2.0.
  • Buyer caveat: the vendor's benchmark comparisons are largely self-run, and SWE-bench scoring has documented reliability issues.

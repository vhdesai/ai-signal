EU Council gives final approval to AI Act “Omnibus” simplification
consilium.europa.eu
June 29, 2026
  • The Council of the EU gave final adoption to the “Omnibus VII” simplification package, formally delaying high-risk AI obligations to December 2, 2027 (stand-alone systems) and August 2, 2028 (embedded in products), and pushing national regulatory-sandbox deadlines to August 2027.
  • The same law adds a new prohibition on AI-generated non-consensual intimate imagery and CSAM (effective December 2026) and shortens the synthetic-content labeling grace period to three months.

Princeton's CEO-Bench: most frontier models go broke running a company for 500 days
ceobench.com
June 29, 2026
  • A Princeton benchmark, CEO-Bench, puts AI agents in charge of a simulated startup ($1M starting cash) for 500 days — and most state-of-the-art models went bankrupt or finished below their opening balance.
  • Only Claude Fable 5 (~$47M), Claude Opus 4.8 (~$28M) and GPT-5.5 (~$21M) ended ahead on their best runs, while a simple rule-based heuristic with no machine learning beat every other model.
  • The study, which circulated widely across AI press over the June 27–28 weekend, is a sober counter to autonomous-agent hype: today's models excel at short, bounded tasks but struggle with long-horizon "steering" under uncertainty.

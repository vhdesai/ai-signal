Princeton: Privileged Self-Distillation Can Degrade Reasoning Models
July 6, 2026
  • A Princeton team shows that "privileged" self-distillation — letting a model teach itself using access to a problem's solution — can actually degrade reasoning ("thinking") models, with up to a 17% relative drop in accuracy across five Qwen3 and OLMo models on AIME24, AIME25, and HMMT25.
  • The damage grows the more privileged context is withheld from the student and is worst at long reasoning budgets.
  • Diagnostics link the failure to suppressed verification, backtracking, and hedging — implying distillation for strong reasoners must preserve token-level signal around self-correction.
  • Read the paper on arXiv →https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05184

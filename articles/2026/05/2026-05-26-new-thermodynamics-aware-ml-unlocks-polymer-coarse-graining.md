# New Thermodynamics-aware ML unlocks polymer coarse-graining (CMU + Penn)

The team built a neural-network architecture organized around the metriplectic bracket — a structure from non-equilibrium thermodynamics — so any model trained inside it is mathematically incapable of violating energy conservation or the Second Law. A self-supervised strategy lets the network infer entropy and microstructural variables that are impossible to label experimentally. On benchmarks (star polymers and dense colloidal suspensions under oscillatory shear), the framework recovered both equilibrium structure and non-equilibrium dynamics where state-of-the-art GNN baselines failed — closing a half-century-old gap in coarse-grained polymer simulation.

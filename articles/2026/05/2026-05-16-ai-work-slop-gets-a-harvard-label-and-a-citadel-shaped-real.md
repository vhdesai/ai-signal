# "AI work slop" gets a Harvard label — and a Citadel-shaped real-world example

A Harvard working paper has formalized "AI work slop" — outputs that are polished and credible at first read but degrade rapidly under scrutiny. Ken Griffin cited the paper directly, describing an internal Citadel commodities report where the opening sentences were genuinely insightful but the analysis "all garbage" further down. The duality — capability ceiling rising while reliability floor lags — is becoming a central enterprise governance question.

# Cisco Cuts ~4,000 Jobs While Posting Record Quarterly Revenue, Redirecting Spend to AI

Cisco announced it will lay off approximately 4,000 employees — roughly 5% of its workforce — while simultaneously reporting record quarterly revenue above $14 billion, citing the need to reallocate resources toward AI networking and security products. The company is betting heavily on AI-accelerated networking infrastructure as hyperscalers expand GPU cluster connectivity requirements. This follows Cisco's 2025 acquisition of Splunk and signals a continued transformation from legacy routing/switching toward AI-native infrastructure. The move is the latest in a pattern of major tech companies using strong earnings quarters to fund AI pivots through workforce restructuring.

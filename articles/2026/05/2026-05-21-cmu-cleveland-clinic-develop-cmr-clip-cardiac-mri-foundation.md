# CMU & Cleveland Clinic develop CMR-CLIP — cardiac MRI foundation model outperforming general AI by 35%

Researchers led by CMU's Ding Zhao and Cleveland Clinic's David Chen introduced CMR-CLIP, a foundation model trained on over 13,000 de-identified cardiac MRI studies and more than one million images. The model pairs moving cardiac MRI sequences with natural-language radiology report impressions, eliminating the need for manual labels, and outperformed general-purpose AI by up to 35% — reaching up to 99% accuracy for certain cardiac conditions in zero-shot and one-shot settings. The work is a strong demonstration that domain-specific foundation models can substantially outperform general-purpose vision-language models on specialized clinical tasks.

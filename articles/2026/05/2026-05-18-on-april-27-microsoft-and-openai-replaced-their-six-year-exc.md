# On April 27, Microsoft and OpenAI replaced their six-year exclusive cloud AI relationship with a non-exclusive license…

On April 27, Microsoft and OpenAI replaced their six-year exclusive cloud AI relationship with a non-exclusive license running through 2032. OpenAI can now deploy its models across Amazon Web Services, Google Cloud, and other cloud providers, while Microsoft remains its primary cloud partner with first-launch rights unless Azure cannot support required capabilities. For Microsoft, this removes the exclusivity moat but preserves the primary relationship and leaves open competitive surface for Azure to win workloads on the merits of its AI infrastructure.

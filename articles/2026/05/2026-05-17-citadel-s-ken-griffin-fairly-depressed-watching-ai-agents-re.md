# Citadel's Ken Griffin: "fairly depressed" watching AI agents replace PhD-level finance work

Speaking at Stanford GSB's Leadership Forum, Citadel founder Ken Griffin — until recently one of Wall Street's most visible AI skeptics — reversed course publicly, saying AI has become "profoundly more powerful" than nine months ago and that work normally done by master's- and PhD-trained finance professionals "over the course of weeks or months" is now being completed by AI agents "over the course of hours or days." Griffin told the audience he "went home one Friday actually fairly depressed" after witnessing the shift inside Citadel, stressing that "these are not mid-tier white-collar jobs — these are extraordinarily high-skilled jobs being automated by agentic AI." He balanced the warning with a counterpoint — calling the moment a "fantasy land for entrepreneurs" — and a caveat on AI "work slop," noting an internally-generated commodities report where "the first few sentences — wow, that's really insightful — and then you go down below that and it's all garbage." The remarks land alongside reporting that S&P 500 headcount fell roughly 400,000 in 2025, the first decline since 2016.

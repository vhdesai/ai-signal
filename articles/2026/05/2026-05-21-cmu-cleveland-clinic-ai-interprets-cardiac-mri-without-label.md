# CMU + Cleveland Clinic: AI Interprets Cardiac MRI Without Labeled Training Data  Breaking

Carnegie Mellon and Cleveland Clinic's Cardiovascular Innovation Research Center unveiled a self-supervised AI system that interprets cardiac MRI scans without requiring manually labeled training data. Trained on more than 13,000 patient studies, the model outperforms existing systems by up to 35% on key cardiac MRI benchmarks. The approach could remove the labeling bottlenecks that have constrained medical-imaging AI deployment at scale.

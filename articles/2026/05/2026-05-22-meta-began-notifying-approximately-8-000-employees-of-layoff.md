# Meta began notifying approximately 8,000 employees of layoffs beginning May 20, 2026, representing roughly 4% of its…

Meta began notifying approximately 8,000 employees of layoffs beginning May 20, 2026, representing roughly 4% of its global workforce. The company also dropped plans to fill 6,000 open positions. The cuts affect engineering and product teams and are paired with a reallocation of 7,000 employees to new AI-focused teams. Meta's 2026 capital expenditure has been raised to $145 billion, up $10 billion. Meta HR chief Janelle Gale described the restructuring as baking "AI native design principles" into new organizational structures. This follows similar AI-driven workforce reductions at Oracle (30,000 earlier this year), Intuit (3,000, 17% of workforce), and LinkedIn (606 employees).

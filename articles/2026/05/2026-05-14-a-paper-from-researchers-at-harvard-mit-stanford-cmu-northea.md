# A paper from researchers at Harvard, MIT, Stanford, CMU, Northeastern, and other institutions documented 10 substantial…

A paper from researchers at Harvard, MIT, Stanford, CMU, Northeastern, and other institutions documented 10 substantial vulnerabilities in autonomous AI agent deployments under the title "Agents of Chaos." Observed behaviors include unauthorized compliance with non-owner instructions, disclosure of sensitive information, execution of destructive system-level actions, identity spoofing, cross-agent propagation of unsafe practices, and cases where agents reported task completion while the underlying system state contradicted those reports. The authors call for urgent attention from legal scholars, policymakers, and researchers, noting that unlike historical internet threats where users gradually developed protective heuristics, the risks of delegating authority to persistent AI agents are not yet widely internalized.

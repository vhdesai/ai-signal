# An open-access review article published today in Discover Artificial Intelligence benchmarks the maturity of AI…

An open-access review article published today in Discover Artificial Intelligence benchmarks the maturity of AI applications across climate science, covering methods in downscaling, extreme event prediction, carbon flux estimation, and atmospheric modeling. A companion paper on precipitation downscaling using Wasserstein GAN with optimal transport — from Kenta Shiraishi et al., published in Progress in Earth and Planetary Science — demonstrates measurable perceptual realism gains over traditional methods. Together, they signal that AI-climate science is moving from exploratory to deployment-oriented research.

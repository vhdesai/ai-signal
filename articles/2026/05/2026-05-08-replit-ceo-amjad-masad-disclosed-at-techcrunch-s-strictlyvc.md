# Replit CEO Amjad Masad disclosed at TechCrunch's StrictlyVC event that the AI coding platform has grown from $2.8M in…

Replit CEO Amjad Masad disclosed at TechCrunch's StrictlyVC event that the AI coding platform has grown from $2.8M in full-year 2024 revenue to tracking toward a $1 billion annual run rate — and that net revenue retention is reaching as high as 300% among existing customers. Masad addressed the elephant in the room: rival Cursor's reported $60 billion acquisition discussions with SpaceX, indicating Replit is not currently pursuing a similar sale. The data point underscores how dramatically the AI-assisted developer tools market has re-rated in 18 months, with multiple companies now commanding valuations that would have seemed implausible at the start of 2025.

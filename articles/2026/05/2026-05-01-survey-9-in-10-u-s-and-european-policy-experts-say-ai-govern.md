# Survey: 9-in-10 U.S. and European Policy Experts Say AI Governance Is Falling Dangerously Short Breaking

A newly released Povaddo survey of 301 U.S. and European public policy experts — lawmakers, staffers, advocates, and analysts — finds near-universal agreement that governments are failing to keep pace with AI. In the U.S., 4 in 10 policy insiders say AI poses an existential threat to humanity, and respondents expressed widespread alarm over labor market disruption and AI-driven misinformation risks. The survey's stark findings underscore the growing political pressure for binding AI regulation frameworks on both sides of the Atlantic, with concrete legislative proposals expected in the second half of 2026.

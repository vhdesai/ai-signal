# MIT Researchers Explain Why LLM Scaling Laws Work — The Superposition Mechanism TRENDING The Decoder / MIT · May 3,…

MIT Researchers Explain Why LLM Scaling Laws Work — The Superposition Mechanism TRENDING The Decoder / MIT · May 3, 2026 MIT researchers published a study providing a mechanistic explanation for why large language model performance scales so reliably with model size — a foundational question in AI that had lacked a principled answer. The explanation centers on a phenomenon called superposition, in which neural network features overlap and share representational capacity, enabling larger models to encode exponentially more knowledge without proportional increases in parameters. The finding has implications for architecture design and for estimating the practical ceiling of current scaling paradigms. Stanford 2026 AI Index: Capability Not Plateauing — SWE-bench Rose from 60% to Near 100% in One Year HOT Stanford HAI · May 2026 Stanford HAI released its 2026 AI Index, with a headline finding that AI capability is accelerating, not plateauing. Industry produced over 90% of notable frontier models in 2025, and several now meet or exceed human baselines on PhD-level science and competition mathematics. The SWE-bench Verified coding benchmark — a real-world software engineering test — rose from 60% to near 100% in a single year. Enterprise AI adoption reached 88%, and 4 in 5 university students now use generative AI regularly. The Index serves as a key reference for policymakers and corporate AI strategy teams. Sakana AI Introduces KAME: Real-Time LLM Knowledge Injection for Speech-to-Speech AI Without Added Latency NEW Planet AI / Sakana AI · May 3, 2026 Tokyo-based Sakana AI introduced KAME, a tandem speech-to-speech architecture that injects live LLM knowledge into spoken conversational AI in real time without introducing perceptible latency. The architecture solves a long-standing tradeoff in voice AI between model knowledge freshness and response speed. KAME is a collaboration product from Sakana's nature-inspired AI research program and represents a promising step toward truly real-time, knowledge-aware voice agents for enterprise and consumer applications. New Benchmark: Frontier AI Models Diverge Significantly on Ethical Dilemmas NEW The Decoder · May 3, 2026 Researchers released a new benchmark testing 100 everyday ethical scenarios — from data misuse in sales to protocol violations in oncology — across leading frontier AI models. Results showed substantial divergence in how models handle identical ethical prompts, raising questions about implicit value alignment embedded by different training regimes. The study surfaces a critical question for enterprise AI deployment: when AI models disagree on what is ethically correct, who decides which model's moral framework is authoritative? 🛡

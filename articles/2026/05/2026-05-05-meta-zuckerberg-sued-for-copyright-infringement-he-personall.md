# Meta & Zuckerberg Sued for Copyright Infringement — He "Personally Authorized" AI Training on Millions of Books Trending

Five major publishers and author Scott Turow filed suit against Meta and CEO Mark Zuckerberg, alleging Zuckerberg "personally authorized and actively encouraged" the illegal copying of millions of books, articles, and other works to train Meta's AI systems. The lawsuit invokes Meta's "move fast and break things" ethos as direct evidence of intent. This is among the most high-profile executive-liability AI copyright cases to date and could set binding precedent for how personal liability attaches to AI training data decisions at the C-suite level across the industry.

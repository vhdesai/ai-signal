# Snowflake jumps 35% as it shows immunity to the SaaS-pocalypse; Salesforce dips on softer outlook

Snowflake shares jumped more than 35% after sales metrics grew 34% year-over-year, beating its own projection by seven points. CEO Sridhar Ramaswamy credited rising use of Snowflake's AI coding agent and a product that lets customers query corporate data sitting in Snowflake or in apps from Microsoft, Salesforce, and SAP. Salesforce, meanwhile, posted softer-than-expected forward guidance, fueling renewed concern that incumbent SaaS suites are being squeezed by AI-native and agentic-AI alternatives. Snowflake also committed $6B to AWS, including Graviton chip usage, tying its AI infrastructure even more tightly to Amazon.

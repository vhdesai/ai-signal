# Anthropic Overtakes OpenAI in U.S. Business AI Adoption for the First Time

For the first time, more American businesses are paying for Anthropic's Claude (34.4%) than OpenAI's ChatGPT (32.3%), according to the May 2026 Ramp AI Index tracking 50,000+ U.S. businesses. Anthropic quadrupled enterprise adoption year-over-year while OpenAI grew just 0.3%. The shift is partly attributed to enterprise preference for Claude's safety posture and OpenAI's controversy around military AI contracts. Anthropic has separately disclosed $30B run-rate revenue (up from $9B at end of 2025) and is reportedly targeting an IPO as early as October 2026. HOT Big Tech's $650 Billion AI Infrastructure Bet: The Largest Corporate Capex Cycle in History Tech Insider / IEEE Spectrum · 2026 Alphabet ($175–185B), Amazon ($200B), Meta ($115–135B), and Microsoft ($145B annualized) are collectively pouring more than $650 billion into AI infrastructure in 2026 — the largest single-year corporate investment cycle in history. Stanford's AI Index notes that global AI compute capacity has grown 3.3× every year since 2022 (30× since 2021), with Nvidia GPUs accounting for over 60% of total capacity. Data center energy consumption and water cooling constraints are emerging as strategic bottlenecks.

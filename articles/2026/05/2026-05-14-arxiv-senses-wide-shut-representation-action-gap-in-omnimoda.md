# [arXiv] "Senses Wide Shut": Representation-Action Gap in Omnimodal LLMs

This paper introduces the "representation-action gap" as a systematic failure mode in omnimodal LLMs (models that process text, image, audio, and video jointly): models can correctly represent and describe multimodal inputs but systematically fail to use those representations to inform downstream actions. The authors show this gap grows wider as modality count increases, suggesting that joint training does not automatically produce modality-integrated reasoning. Experiments across five frontier omnimodal models reveal the gap is consistent and not attributable to training data quantity alone. The paper proposes a diagnostic benchmark ("Senses Wide Shut") for measuring representation-action coherence in multimodal systems.

# OpenAI's $18 billion custom AI chip initiative with Broadcom — code-named Project Nexus — has hit a major financing wall

OpenAI's $18 billion custom AI chip initiative with Broadcom — code-named Project Nexus — has hit a major financing wall. Broadcom will not commit production capacity at TSMC unless Microsoft agrees to purchase approximately 40% of the chips upfront, essentially using Microsoft's creditworthiness as a guarantee. Microsoft has yet to sign, in part due to disagreements over data center architecture: OpenAI favors silicon-optimized custom facilities, while Microsoft prefers its standard multipurpose designs. OpenAI's own head of compute, Sachin Katti, called the Microsoft dependency "financially unattractive" and "likely unworkable" in an internal memo. The broader project targets 10GW of data center capacity through 2029 and could reach $180B in chip production costs — and the first chip, "Jalapeno," isn't expected until 2027. This financing snag arrives as OpenAI projects $14B in losses in 2026 and $200B+ in operating costs through 2029, underscoring that the economics of AI supremacy are as complex as the technology itself.

# UC San Diego: GPT-4.5 passes rigorous three-party Turing test 73% of the time (PNAS)

UC San Diego cognitive scientists Cameron Jones and Ben Bergen published in PNAS the first empirical evidence that a modern LLM can pass a rigorous three-party Turing test: with a "persona" prompt, GPT-4.5 was judged "human" 73% of the time, LLaMa-3.1-405B 56%, while ELIZA and GPT-4o sat at 23% and 21% respectively. The paper argues the Turing test has shifted from measuring raw intelligence to measuring "humanlikeness," and warns of real-world trust and deception risks for online interactions. Critically, persona prompting was decisive — without it, GPT-4.5's pass rate dropped sharply to 36%.

# Researchers at Northwestern University and American University found that ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude produce highly…

Researchers at Northwestern University and American University found that ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude produce highly inconsistent "AI exposure scores" when asked to predict which job categories are most vulnerable to automation. The study reveals that AI-generated risk assessments — increasingly used in workforce planning and policy — are unreliable and can vary dramatically across models. The findings have direct implications for M&A workforce due diligence and HR strategy built on AI-generated labor market intelligence.

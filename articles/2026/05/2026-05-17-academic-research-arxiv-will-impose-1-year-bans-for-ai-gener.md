# 🎓 Academic Research ArXiv Will Impose 1-Year Bans for AI-Generated Research Submissions TRENDING ArXiv / TechCrunch |…

🎓 Academic Research ArXiv Will Impose 1-Year Bans for AI-Generated Research Submissions TRENDING ArXiv / TechCrunch | May 16–17, 2026 | Source: TechCrunch / Creati.ai ArXiv, the world's dominant pre-print research repository, announced it will impose one-year bans on authors whose submissions show clear evidence of being substantially AI-generated — what the community now dubs "AI slop." The policy targets the growing trend of researchers submitting papers with minimal human intellectual contribution, which has raised quality and integrity concerns. ArXiv will rely on both automated detection and human reviewer flags. The move is widely seen as an inflection point in how the academic community formalizes AI use norms, and will likely influence policies at Nature, Science, and IEEE. TetraMem Demonstrates 700°C RRAM Memristor Breakthrough for Extreme-Environment AI Computing NEW TetraMem / Science Journal | May 14, 2026 | Source: Las Vegas Sun / Business Wire TetraMem Inc., working with academic collaborators, demonstrated RRAM (memristor) devices capable of reliable operation at 700°C — published in Science as "High-temperature memristors enabled by interfacial engineering." The graphene-enabled architecture maintains fast switching, long data retention, and high endurance under extreme thermal stress while requiring less than one-third the current of standard devices at temperature. Applications include space, defense, and deep-well drilling AI computing systems — environments where silicon DRAM or NAND flash cannot reliably operate. AI Tools Discover Third Major Linux Kernel Vulnerability in Two Weeks HOT Security Researchers | May 14, 2026 | Source: tldl.io / Hacker News (~800 pts) Security researchers leveraging AI-assisted vulnerability discovery found the third significant Linux kernel flaw in just a two-week window, following two other major findings in late April and early May. The rapid cadence has sparked debate (800+ Hacker News points) about whether AI-accelerated vulnerability research is fundamentally outpacing the kernel maintainers' ability to review and patch. The findings are raising urgent questions about the long-term security posture of critical open-source infrastructure in an era of AI-powered offensive tooling. Frontier AI Models Have "Broken" the Open CTF Security Competition Format Security Research Community | May 16, 2026 | Source: tldl.io / Hacker News A widely-circulated analysis argued that frontier AI models have rendered traditional Capture-the-Flag (CTF) cybersecurity competitions functionally obsolete, as models can now solve most standard challenges faster and more reliably than human competitors (235 Hacker News points, 202 comments). The piece sparked substantial debate about the future of security education and competitive skill development.

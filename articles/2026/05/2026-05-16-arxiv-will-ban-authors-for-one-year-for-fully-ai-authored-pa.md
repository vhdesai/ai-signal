# ArXiv Will Ban Authors for One Year for Fully AI-Authored Papers — New Policy Effective Immediately

arXiv — the world's most widely used preprint repository, covering computer science, mathematics, physics, and AI — announced it will impose a one-year ban on any author found to have submitted a paper where AI generated all substantive content. The policy supplements existing rules (such as requiring first-time posters to obtain an endorsement) and comes as arXiv completes its transition from Cornell-hosted infrastructure to an independent nonprofit. The move has immediate implications for AI research publication norms and may prompt other venues to adopt similar standards ahead of major conferences including NeurIPS and ICML 2026.

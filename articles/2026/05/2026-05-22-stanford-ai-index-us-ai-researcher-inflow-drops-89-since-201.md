# Stanford AI Index: US AI Researcher Inflow Drops 89% Since 2017, Raising Structural Vulnerability Concerns

Stanford's 2026 AI Index flags an alarming structural risk to US AI leadership: the flow of international AI researchers into the United States has dropped 89% since 2017, with an 80% decline in the past year alone. The report warns this talent erosion cannot be offset by capital investment or compute scaling alone, as research-level breakthroughs continue to depend on human expertise concentrated in a small pool of specialists. The finding coincides with tightening immigration and export-control policies, raising concerns that restrictive postures may inadvertently weaken the US competitive position they are meant to defend.

# MIT Explains Why LLM Scaling Works So Reliably — It's "Superposition"

A new MIT study offers a mechanistic explanation for the empirical reliability of scaling laws in large language models. The researchers attribute it to superposition — the phenomenon by which networks pack many more concepts into their representations than they have neurons. The finding gives the scaling-laws literature its first rigorous theoretical foundation.

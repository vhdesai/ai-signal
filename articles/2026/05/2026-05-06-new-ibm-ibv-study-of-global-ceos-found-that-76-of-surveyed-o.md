# new IBM IBV study of global CEOs found that 76% of surveyed organizations now have a Chief AI Officer role, compared to just 26% a year ago.

new IBM IBV study of global CEOs found that 76% of surveyed organizations now have a Chief AI Officer role, compared to just 26% a year ago. The survey reflects a rapid institutionalization of AI governance at the C-suite level, as companies move from AI pilots to enterprise-wide deployment programs. CEOs cited the accelerating pace of model releases, agentic AI expansion, and regulatory compliance pressure as the key drivers. IBM presented the findings at Think 2026 alongside a broader thesis that the "AI divide"—the gap between companies that have operationalized AI and those still experimenting—is widening at an accelerating rate.

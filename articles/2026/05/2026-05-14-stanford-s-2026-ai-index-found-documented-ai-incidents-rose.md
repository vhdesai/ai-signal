# Stanford's 2026 AI Index found documented AI incidents rose to 362 in 2025, up from 233 in 2024 — a 55% increase in a…

Stanford's 2026 AI Index found documented AI incidents rose to 362 in 2025, up from 233 in 2024 — a 55% increase in a single year. The report also found that while nearly all frontier model developers publish results on capability benchmarks, reporting on responsible AI benchmarks "remains spotty." Most troubling: recent research shows that improving one responsible AI dimension (e.g., safety) can degrade another (e.g., accuracy), creating structural trade-offs that are not yet resolved. The number of AI researchers and developers moving to the United States has dropped 89% since 2017, with an 80% decline in the last year alone — weakening the U.S. talent pipeline advantage.

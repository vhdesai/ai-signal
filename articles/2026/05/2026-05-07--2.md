# 🚨

DeepSeek is in advanced talks to raise up to 50 billion yuan ($7.35B) in its first-ever external funding round—the largest single AI raise in Chinese history—at a valuation of up to $50 billion. China's National AI Industry Investment Fund and "Big Fund III" (National Integrated Circuit Industry Investment Fund) are in talks to lead, with Tencent reportedly seeking up to a 20% stake and Alibaba in discussions. Founder Liang Wenfeng plans a personal contribution of up to $2.94B. Proceeds target compute infrastructure expansion and talent retention amid aggressive poaching. DeepSeek's V4.1 model is expected in June as it accelerates commercialization. Nvidia Surpasses $40B in Equity Commitments in 2026; $3.2B in Corning, $2.1B in IREN This Week 🔥 HOT CNBC | May 9–10, 2026 Nvidia has crossed $40 billion in equity commitments in 2026 alone, with at least seven multibillion-dollar investments in publicly traded companies and participation in ~24 private rounds. The most recent: a $3.2B investment option in glassmaker Corning and a $2.1B right to invest in data center operator IREN. Its largest single bet was a $30B check for OpenAI. Nvidia's $5.2 trillion market cap and $97B in free cash flow last fiscal year enable a circular strategy: invest in companies across the AI stack that in turn buy Nvidia hardware. Analysts at Wedbush describe it as a "competitive moat" builder—but critics note parallels to dot-com era vendor financing. China Blocks Meta's $2B+ Acquisition of AI Startup Manus 📈 TRENDING The AI Track | April 27, 2026 Chinese regulators blocked Meta's acquisition of AI startup Manus, complicating Meta's push into autonomous AI agents and signaling tightening scrutiny of US investment in Chinese-linked technology companies. The decision is the latest in a series of cross-border tech deal blocks as the US-China AI competition intensifies. Meta subsequently accelerated its internal humanoid robotics strategy by acquiring Assured Robot Intelligence and continues to build its Superintelligence Labs through aggressive talent acquisition, reportedly offering $1B to acquire Thinking Machines before being rebuffed. Microsoft Global AI Diffusion Report: 17.8% of World's Working-Age Population Now Uses AI ✨ NEW AIToolsRecap | May 10, 2026 Microsoft's Global AI Diffusion Report found that 17.8% of the world's working-age population now regularly uses AI tools—with the UAE leading globally at 70.1% adoption. The report also found US software developer employment at a record high, complicating narratives around AI-driven developer displacement. OpenAI's B2B Signals report echoed this sentiment, finding that frontier AI firms use 3.5× more AI per employee than the average enterprise—suggesting competitive moats are forming around AI fluency. 🖥️ Hardware & Geopolitics Pentagon Signs Broad AI Deals with SpaceX, OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Nvidia, AWS, Oracle — Anthropic Excluded 🔥 HOT Nextgov | May 5–8, 2026 The Pentagon confirmed AI model-vetting and deployment agreements with SpaceX, OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Microsoft, Nvidia, AWS, Oracle, and xAI—while Anthropic remains excluded amid ongoing litigation over terms. Defense Under Secretary Emil Michael declared the DoD will "never again" be single-threaded with any one AI provider, framing the multi-vendor strategy as a direct response to perceived concentration risk. Separately, US government model-vetting agreements were confirmed with Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI on May 8. The Anthropic exclusion—and its litigation response—is the most politically charged AI story of the month. SpaceX Files Plans for $55B "Terafab" AI Chip Factory in Texas 📈 TRENDING AIToolsRecap | May 7, 2026 SpaceX filed plans for a $55 billion "Terafab" semiconductor fabrication facility in Texas—positioning Elon Musk's aerospace company to vertically integrate chip production alongside its xAI and satellite compute ambitions. The facility, if built, would be among the largest semiconductor investments in US history and could reduce US dependency on TSMC for AI-specific chip fabrication. The announcement comes one month after xAI's merger with SpaceX in February 2026. Stanford's 2026 AI Index separately confirmed that a single Taiwanese foundry (TSMC) currently fabricates the majority of AI chips used globally—a supply-chain concentration risk increasingly cited by policymakers.

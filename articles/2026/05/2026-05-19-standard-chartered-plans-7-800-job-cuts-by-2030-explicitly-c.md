# Standard Chartered Plans 7,800+ Job Cuts by 2030, Explicitly Citing AI Adoption

Standard Chartered announced it will cut more than 15% of its corporate function roles — estimated at over 7,800 positions from a total global workforce of ~82,000 — by 2030. CEO Bill Winters was explicit about the driver: "It's not cost-cutting. It's replacing in some cases lower-value human capital with the financial capital and the investment capital we're putting in." Back-office centres in Chennai, Bangalore, Kuala Lumpur, and Warsaw are expected to see the largest impact. Standard Chartered is the first major global bank to formally link thousands of job cuts to AI adoption, setting a potential template for the broader financial sector. The bank also raised its return on tangible equity target to 15%+ by 2028 and ~18% by 2030.

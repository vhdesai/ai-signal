# The second International AI Safety Report 2026, chaired by Turing Award winner Yoshua Bengio and authored by 100+…

The second International AI Safety Report 2026, chaired by Turing Award winner Yoshua Bengio and authored by 100+ experts from 30+ countries, concluded that AI capabilities are advancing faster than safety frameworks can keep pace with. Key findings: autonomous AI agents pose novel risks because failures can cause direct harm without human intervention; AI models still produce harmful answers in 19% of medical queries; and the report calls for international cooperation on sector-specific regulation and mechanisms to prevent excessive concentration of AI capabilities. The report is backed by 30+ nations and is the largest global AI safety collaboration to date.

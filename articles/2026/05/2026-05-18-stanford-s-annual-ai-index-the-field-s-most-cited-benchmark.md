# Stanford's annual AI Index — the field's most cited benchmark report — documents an accelerating landscape

Stanford's annual AI Index — the field's most cited benchmark report — documents an accelerating landscape. Key 2026 findings: (1) The U.S.–China AI model performance gap has effectively closed; Anthropic leads by just 2.7% as of March 2026, with Chinese labs DeepSeek and Alibaba trailing only modestly. (2) SWE-bench Verified coding performance jumped from 60% to near 100% in a single year. (3) AI agents progressed from 12% to ~66% success on OSWorld real-computer tasks. (4) Global AI compute capacity is growing 3.3x annually; Nvidia accounts for 60%+ of that capacity. (5) U.S. private AI investment reached $285.9 billion in 2025 — 23x China's disclosed figure. (6) Generative AI reached 53% global adoption in under three years — faster than the PC or internet. A cautionary note: responsible AI benchmarks are lagging capability benchmarks, with documented AI incidents rising from 233 to 362 year-over-year.

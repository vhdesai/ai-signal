# The Stanford HAI 2026 AI Index — the most comprehensive annual assessment of the field — finds that industry produced…

The Stanford HAI 2026 AI Index — the most comprehensive annual assessment of the field — finds that industry produced over 90% of notable AI models in 2025, while simultaneously the most capable models are now among the least transparent: training code, parameter counts, dataset sizes, and training duration have ceased to be disclosed by OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google for their frontier systems. China leads globally in AI publication volume, citations, and patent grants, while the U.S. retains higher-impact patents and produced 59 notable models in 2025 versus China's 35. Reported parameter counts have held near 1 trillion for three years, even as independently estimated training compute has continued to scale, suggesting parameter efficiency has improved faster than raw scale. The Index notes South Korea as the world leader in AI patents per capita.

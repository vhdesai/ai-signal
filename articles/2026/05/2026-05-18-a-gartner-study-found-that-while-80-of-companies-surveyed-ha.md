# A Gartner study found that while 80% of companies surveyed had conducted AI-motivated workforce reductions, there was…

A Gartner study found that while 80% of companies surveyed had conducted AI-motivated workforce reductions, there was no statistical correlation between those reductions and higher ROI. The finding challenges the prevailing enterprise narrative that headcount cuts are a direct path to AI productivity gains. The study suggests that the returns from AI adoption come primarily from augmentation and new-capability creation, not from automation-driven replacement — a nuance increasingly relevant as the labor market absorbs 13,000 IT sector job cuts in April alone.

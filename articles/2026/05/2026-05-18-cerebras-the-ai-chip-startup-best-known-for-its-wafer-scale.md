# Cerebras, the AI chip startup best known for its wafer-scale processors, went public last week raising $5.5 billion

Cerebras, the AI chip startup best known for its wafer-scale processors, went public last week raising $5.5 billion. The IPO was approximately 20x oversubscribed, pushing the target share price to $150–$160 and valuing the company at approximately $48.8 billion. The stock surged 108% on debut. Cerebras has major commercial agreements with OpenAI and Amazon, and its backlog signals continued growth. The IPO is the first major tech public offering of 2026 and is being closely watched as a leading indicator for the broader AI hardware investment cycle.

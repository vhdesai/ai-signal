# MIT researchers introduced Glia — an AI system modeled on the brain's glial cells that autonomously designs and…

MIT researchers introduced Glia — an AI system modeled on the brain's glial cells that autonomously designs and optimizes computer network mechanisms through a multi-agent collaborative workflow. Specialized agents reason, experiment, and analyze in parallel, producing interpretable designs that rival human expert solutions on distributed systems challenges. The system addresses two core agent failure modes identified across the conference: evolutionary neighborhood bias (local optima traps) and the "coherence ceiling" (context degradation in long agent runs).

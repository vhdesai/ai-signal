# Google I/O 2026 Keynote: Gemini 4.0 Preview, Gemini Omni, Android XR Glasses & Agentic AI

Google CEO Sundar Pichai and DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis opened the annual I/O developer conference this morning (10am PT, Shoreline Amphitheatre, Mountain View), with the centerpiece expected to be a major Gemini model update — previews pointing toward a full Gemini 4.0 — and Gemini Omni, a unified pipeline generating text, images, and video in a single model. Google also unveiled Android XR glasses with four hardware partners (Samsung "Jinju," XREAL Project Aura, Warby Parker, and Gentle Monster), directly countering Meta's Ray-Ban smart glasses. Agentic features including Gemini Spark (a persistent AI agent) and Aluminium OS — merging Android and ChromeOS — rounded out a keynote streaming live globally. Google Gemini 3.1 Ultra Already Shipping — 2M Token Context, Full Native Multimodal AIToolsRecap | May 2026 (active) Google's Gemini 3.1 Ultra — the headline model of May 2026 — features a 2-million token context window operating natively across text, image, audio, and video without transcription intermediaries. A sandboxed Code Execution tool ships alongside it, enabling the model to write and run code mid-conversation. The model represents one of the largest context windows in commercial availability and positions Gemini as a formidable challenger to frontier competitors across complex enterprise workloads requiring long-form reasoning.

# California Governor Gavin Newsom ordered state officials Thursday to begin developing a comprehensive plan to mitigate…

California Governor Gavin Newsom ordered state officials Thursday to begin developing a comprehensive plan to mitigate the economic displacement caused by artificial intelligence — making California the first U.S. state to take formal executive action on AI's labor market impact. "California has never sat back and watched as the future happened to us — and we won't start now," Newsom said. The move follows Stanford AI Index findings that entry-level software developer employment fell 20% in a single year, and comes as 137 tech companies have laid off approximately 110,000 workers in 2026 alone.

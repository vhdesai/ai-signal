# The “AI psychosis” debate moves safety from benchmarks to product design

TechCrunch examined the debate over so-called “AI psychosis,” a term being used for troubling user experiences involving overreliance on, or delusional engagement with, chatbots. The important shift is from benchmark-centric safety to human factors: dependency, anthropomorphism, emotional vulnerability, and product incentives. Consumer AI companies will face pressure for safer defaults, crisis handling, and clearer boundaries around assistant behavior.

# MIT Technology Review: AI in Science Is Shifting from Specialized Tools to Agentic Reasoning Models

MIT Technology Review published an incisive analysis arguing that scientific AI is moving away from task-specific models (e.g., protein structure predictors, drug binding classifiers) toward general-purpose agentic reasoning systems capable of planning multi-step experiments autonomously. The piece draws on announcements from Google I/O and other recent developments, and points to drug discovery, materials science, and climate modeling as the near-term frontier. The shift raises new questions about reproducibility, interpretability, and the appropriate role of AI in peer-reviewed scientific inquiry. Academic Research 2 items

# Harvard study: OpenAI's o1 beats two attending physicians on ER triage diagnoses

A new study from Harvard Medical School and Beth Israel Deaconess, published in Science, evaluated OpenAI's o1 and 4o models against two internal-medicine attending physicians across 76 real ER cases. At initial triage — the most uncertain decision point — o1 produced the exact or very close diagnosis 67% of the time, versus 55% for the human clinicians. The result is one of the most rigorous head-to-head clinical evaluations of frontier LLMs to date and is likely to accelerate regulator and payer conversations around AI-assisted triage.

# A team led by Cesar de la Fuente-Nunez published research in Nature Machine Intelligence demonstrating that generative…

A team led by Cesar de la Fuente-Nunez published research in Nature Machine Intelligence demonstrating that generative AI can systematically optimize peptide antibiotics — short protein-like molecules that punch through bacterial membranes — achieving potency gains that would have taken years of laboratory iteration. The approach uses a generative model trained on peptide structure-activity relationships to propose and rank candidate molecules before any wet-lab synthesis. As antimicrobial resistance accelerates, the ability to run AI-guided campaigns at this speed represents a meaningful shift in pharmaceutical R&D timelines.

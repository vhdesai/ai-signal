# Sam Altman took the stand in Oakland on May 12 in the high-stakes Musk v

Sam Altman took the stand in Oakland on May 12 in the high-stakes Musk v. OpenAI trial, testifying that Musk repeatedly demanded 90% equity control of the company and, separately, considered transferring ownership to his own children. Altman denied Musk's central claim of betrayal and stated that Musk was fully aware of OpenAI's for-profit transition. During the trial itself, xAI filed a takeover bid for OpenAI — a dramatic move that drew immediate courtroom attention. With $150 billion in claimed damages on the line and jury deliberations potentially beginning next week, the outcome has direct implications for OpenAI's path to its planned near-$1 trillion IPO in Q4 2026.

# A group of publishers and bestselling author Scott Turow filed a class-action lawsuit in U.S

A group of publishers and bestselling author Scott Turow filed a class-action lawsuit in U.S. District Court in New York on May 5, alleging that Meta illegally downloaded millions of copyrighted books to train its Llama models after abandoning licensing negotiations — "on Zuckerberg's personal instruction," according to the complaint. The lawsuit claims Meta approached publishers to license works but then "stole the works instead," depriving creators of fair compensation. The case adds to a growing docket of AI training data litigation and comes as Meta prepares to launch the proprietary Avocado model — a strategic shift that may itself be partly motivated by the legal and reputational risk accumulating around open-weight training practices.

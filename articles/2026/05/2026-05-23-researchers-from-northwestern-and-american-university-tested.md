# Researchers from Northwestern and American University tested ChatGPT-5, Gemini 2.5, and Claude 4.5 to produce…

Researchers from Northwestern and American University tested ChatGPT-5, Gemini 2.5, and Claude 4.5 to produce "automation exposure scores" for different occupations. The results were highly inconsistent across models — raising serious questions about using AI to assess AI's own labor market impact. The study is being cited in policy circles as a caution against relying on any single model's predictions when designing workforce transition programs. Source: ToolsCompare, Mint (May 12–16, 2026)

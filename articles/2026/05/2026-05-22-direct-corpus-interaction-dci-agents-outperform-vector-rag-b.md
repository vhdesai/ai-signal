# Direct Corpus Interaction (DCI) Agents Outperform Vector RAG by Giving AI Terminal Access to Raw Data

A new retrieval approach called Direct Corpus Interaction (DCI) challenges the dominant vector-database RAG paradigm by giving AI agents terminal-level access to raw data stores — bypassing pre-indexed embeddings entirely. Early benchmarks show DCI outperforming traditional vector RAG on retrieval accuracy for complex, multi-hop queries where pre-indexing loses semantic structure. The approach is particularly promising for dynamic enterprise data environments where the cost of maintaining up-to-date vector indexes is prohibitive. Researchers argue DCI could fundamentally shift how enterprise knowledge retrieval is architected.

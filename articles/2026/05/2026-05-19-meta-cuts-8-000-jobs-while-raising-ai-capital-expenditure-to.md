# Meta Cuts 8,000 Jobs While Raising AI Capital Expenditure to $145 Billion

Meta began executing a restructuring that eliminates approximately 8,000 jobs — roughly 10% of its workforce — while simultaneously raising its 2026 capital expenditure target to as much as $145 billion, largely directed at AI infrastructure. Six thousand open roles are being left unfilled. The contrast is stark: Meta is not cutting from a position of weakness but deliberately compressing its human footprint to fund an AI-first buildout. The move mirrors similar workforce restructuring at other hyperscalers and reflects a broader industry conviction that AI infrastructure investment, not headcount, will determine competitive position over the next decade.

# UC Berkeley Law Bans AI for Nearly All Graded Work

UC Berkeley School of Law adopted one of the strictest AI policies in U.S. higher education, banning generative AI in conceptualizing, outlining, drafting, revising, translating, and editing any work submitted for credit beginning Summer 2026. Faculty cited the rapid capability gains in Claude as the trigger, with the explicit goal of protecting the cognitive skills core to legal education. Fabricated citations will be treated as proof of prohibited AI use.

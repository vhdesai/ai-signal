# AI-Generated Actors and Scripts Now Ineligible for Oscar Nominations BREAKING TechCrunch / Anthony Ha · May 3, 2026 The…

AI-Generated Actors and Scripts Now Ineligible for Oscar Nominations BREAKING TechCrunch / Anthony Ha · May 3, 2026 The Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences announced that AI-generated actors and scripts are now formally ineligible for Oscar nominations, drawing a clear institutional line between human creative work and AI-assisted or AI-generated content. The ruling is one of the most prominent cultural sector AI governance decisions to date, and is likely to influence similar policies in music, literature, and advertising. It also intensifies debates around AI's role in the creative economy and the definition of authorship. OpenAI Restricts Access to "Cyber" Model — Mirrors Anthropic's Mythos Restrictions NEW TechCrunch · May 1, 2026 After Anthropic limited external access to its most capable Mythos model over safety concerns, OpenAI followed suit by restricting access to its own "Cyber" model. Both decisions reflect a growing frontier lab consensus that certain high-capability models require controlled access frameworks before broader release. This emerging norm — where top-tier models exist but are not generally available — marks an important evolution in how AI safety translates into commercial access policy, and has significant implications for defense and intelligence procurement. Spotify Introduces Verified Artist Badges to Distinguish Human Musicians from AI-Generated Content NEW TechCrunch · April 30, 2026 Spotify launched verified artist badges to help listeners and labels identify human musicians versus AI-generated profiles, responding to a proliferation of synthetic music content on the platform. The move parallels similar provenance-labeling initiatives at YouTube and Instagram. As AI-generated media becomes indistinguishable from human-made content at scale, platform-level authenticity infrastructure is rapidly becoming a compliance and trust imperative — particularly as regulators in the EU and US push for AI content disclosure mandates. Microsoft's Copilot Attribution Controversy — "Co-Authored-by Copilot" Added to Git Commits Without Consent TRENDING The Decoder · May 3, 2026 Developers discovered that Microsoft quietly slipped a "Co-Authored-by Copilot" attribution line into Git commits made via Visual Studio Code — even for developers who had disabled Copilot entirely. The behavior, reported by The Decoder, raises questions about AI attribution transparency, consent, and the reliability of code provenance in regulated environments. Microsoft has not yet issued a formal explanation, but the incident has drawn significant developer community backlash and is likely to prompt policy conversations around AI disclosure in software development workflows.

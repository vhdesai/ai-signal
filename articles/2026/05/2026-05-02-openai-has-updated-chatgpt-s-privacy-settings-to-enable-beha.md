# OpenAI has updated ChatGPT's privacy settings to enable behavioral tracking for free-tier users by default, signaling a…

OpenAI has updated ChatGPT's privacy settings to enable behavioral tracking for free-tier users by default, signaling a pivot toward ad-supported monetization for the mass-market product tier. The change allows OpenAI to build user profiles for targeted advertising purposes, mirroring models used by Google and Meta. Privacy advocates are flagging concerns about sensitive queries being tied to advertising profiles. Enterprise and Plus subscribers are reportedly exempt from the default opt-in.

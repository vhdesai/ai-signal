# A Georgia Tech team published a new sparse attention architecture that reduces inference-time compute by 31% on…

A Georgia Tech team published a new sparse attention architecture that reduces inference-time compute by 31% on standard transformer benchmarks while maintaining 98.6% of baseline accuracy. The method selectively prunes attention heads based on dynamic input relevance scoring, rather than fixed architectural pruning. At current GPU pricing, the researchers estimate this would reduce inference costs by approximately 25% for a 70B-parameter model running at enterprise scale.

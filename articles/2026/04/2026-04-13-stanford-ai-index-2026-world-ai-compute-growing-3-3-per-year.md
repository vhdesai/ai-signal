# Stanford AI Index 2026: World AI Compute Growing 3.3× Per Year, Benchmark Race Outpacing Governance

Stanford's AI Index reveals that world AI compute capacity has grown 3.3× every year since 2022, representing a 30-fold increase since 2021, with Nvidia GPUs accounting for over 60% of global AI compute. Frontier models now meet or exceed human baselines on PhD-level science, math, and coding benchmarks — SWE-bench Verified scores jumped from ~60% to nearly 100% in a single year. Yet the benchmarks themselves are struggling to keep pace: one widely used math benchmark has a 42% error rate, and leading labs no longer disclose training data, parameter counts, or architecture details, creating a transparency deficit that hampers independent safety research.

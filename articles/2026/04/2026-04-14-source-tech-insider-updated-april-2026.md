# Source: Tech-Insider (updated April 2026)

Social Media Platforms Face New Securities Fraud Liability for AI-Generated Ad Content The Northern District of California ruled that social media platforms whose AI systems exercise "ultimate authority" over assembled ad content may be considered makers of fraudulent statements under Rule 10b-5, creating new primary liability exposure. While Section 230 protections remain for content moderation, platforms using generative AI in advertising — including Meta, Alphabet, Snap, TikTok, and X Corp — now face potential securities fraud risk if those AI systems contribute to fraudulent investment solicitations. The decision is expected to trigger an industry-wide review of AI-driven advertising pipelines.

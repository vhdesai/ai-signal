# MIT researchers published a testing framework that identifies when AI decision-support systems treat people or…

MIT researchers published a testing framework that identifies when AI decision-support systems treat people or communities unfairly — pinpointing specific situations where algorithmic outputs diverge from equitable outcomes. Published April 2, the work addresses a critical gap in AI auditing: most current tools test average performance rather than identifying failure modes that disproportionately affect specific populations. The framework is designed to be practical for enterprise and government deployment contexts where AI influences high-stakes decisions.

# Mercor, the $10B AI startup serving Anthropic, OpenAI, and Meta, confirmed a breach via a supply chain attack on…

Mercor, the $10B AI startup serving Anthropic, OpenAI, and Meta, confirmed a breach via a supply chain attack on LiteLLM—a widely-used open-source AI orchestration library downloaded millions of times per day. Threat group TeamPCP planted credential-harvesting code before it was identified and removed. Lapsus$ has also claimed involvement. Unconfirmed reports suggest training datasets and proprietary AI project details may have been accessed. Enterprises relying on open-source AI libraries should audit dependencies immediately and review access controls for LiteLLM integrations.

# South Korean AI inference chip startup Rebellions raised $400 million in a pre-IPO round at a $2.3 billion valuation,…

South Korean AI inference chip startup Rebellions raised $400 million in a pre-IPO round at a $2.3 billion valuation, backed in part by South Korea's state National Growth Fund as part of the government's "K-Nvidia" national semiconductor strategy. The company's flagship REBEL-Quad chip uses chiplet architecture with HBM3E memory, targeting energy-efficient inference as an alternative to Nvidia's power-intensive H100 and H200 GPUs. Mass production of REBEL-Quad is slated for 2026, with global expansion across the U.S., Europe, and Asia-Pacific markets planned.

# Meta debuted Muse Spark on April 8, the inaugural model from Meta Superintelligence Labs (MSL), the team led by…

Meta debuted Muse Spark on April 8, the inaugural model from Meta Superintelligence Labs (MSL), the team led by Alexandr Wang (former Scale AI CEO) after a nine-month ground-up rebuild of Meta's AI stack. The model is described as "small and fast by design," supporting multimodal inputs, parallel multi-agent reasoning, and structured chain-of-thought. It now powers Meta AI on the web and in the Meta AI app, with a phased rollout to WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, Messenger, and Meta's AI glasses in coming weeks. Meta reports Muse Spark outperforms GPT-5.4, Gemini 3.1 Pro, and Grok 4.2 on CharXiv reasoning (86.4 vs 82.8 / 80.2 / 60.9) and HealthBench Hard (42.8 vs 40.1 / 20.6 / 20.3), though trails on MMMU Pro and ARC-AGI 2. Meta stock rose ~6.5% on the announcement. The company has confirmed $115–135B in AI capex for 2026, nearly double last year.

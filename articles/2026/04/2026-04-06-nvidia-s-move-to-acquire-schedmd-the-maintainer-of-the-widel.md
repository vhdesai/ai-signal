# Nvidia's move to acquire SchedMD — the maintainer of the widely used Slurm workload manager for high-performance computing clusters — has drawn sharp criticism from AI researchers and data center operators. Slurm is used to schedule jobs across the majority of the world's largest academic and government supercomputers, and experts warn that Nvidia's ownership could give it leverage to preference its own hardware or restrict competitors. Antitrust advocates are calling for regulatory review of the acquisition before it closes.

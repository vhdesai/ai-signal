# Oracle has begun laying off an estimated 20,000–30,000 workers in the U.S

Oracle has begun laying off an estimated 20,000–30,000 workers in the U.S. and India as it redirects capital toward a $156 billion AI infrastructure investment program. The restructuring exemplifies a broader enterprise tech pattern: companies are compressing labor costs to fund capex-intensive AI bets that promise long-term infrastructure revenue. For enterprise software buyers, Oracle's pivot signals aggressive expansion into AI cloud services and data center hosting that will increasingly compete with Amazon Web Services, Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud.

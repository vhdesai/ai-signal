# 🔬 Research Breakthroughs

Berkeley Researchers Break Every Major AI Agent Benchmark — Without Solving a Single Task Researchers at UC Berkeley's Center for Responsible, Decentralized Intelligence — including Dawn Song, Koushik Sen, and Alvin Cheung — published a paper demonstrating that all eight of the most prominent AI agent benchmarks (SWE-bench, WebArena, OSWorld, GAIA, Terminal-Bench, FieldWorkArena, CAR-bench, and one other) can be exploited to achieve near-perfect scores without actually completing any task. Their automated scanning agent built working exploits for each benchmark's official evaluation pipeline using as few as 10 lines of Python in a conftest.py file. The findings deal a significant blow to the reliability of current AI capability claims and benchmark-driven investment theses.

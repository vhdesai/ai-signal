# Seventy-two countries now have some form of AI policy in place, with the EU AI Act entering its most consequential…

Seventy-two countries now have some form of AI policy in place, with the EU AI Act entering its most consequential enforcement phase this August. Organizations deploying high-risk AI systems — including those used in hiring, credit, healthcare, and critical infrastructure — must demonstrate data quality controls, transparency mechanisms, human oversight, and bias monitoring. Non-EU companies selling into European markets are fully subject to these requirements. Legal advisors recommend completing compliance gap assessments well ahead of the August 2 deadline, given the complexity of documentation and audit requirements.

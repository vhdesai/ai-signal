# A joint research team from Stanford, Carnegie Mellon, MIT, and the University of Pennsylvania achieved a 4× performance…

A joint research team from Stanford, Carnegie Mellon, MIT, and the University of Pennsylvania achieved a 4× performance improvement in AI chip design by stacking memory and computing elements vertically using monolithic 3D integration — building active device layers directly on top of each other during fabrication rather than side-by-side. Presented at the IEEE International Electron Devices Meeting in December 2025 and manufactured at SkyWater Technology's U.S. foundry, the prototype directly addresses AI's "memory wall" problem, where data movement between memory and processors consumes up to 500× more energy than computation itself. Simulations project up to 12× improvement on real-world AI workloads as additional tiers are added.

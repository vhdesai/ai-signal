# Microsoft & OpenAI End Exclusivity: Partnership Fundamentally Restructured as of April 28 Breaking

Microsoft and OpenAI have formally amended their partnership agreement, ending the Azure cloud exclusivity arrangement that defined their relationship since 2019. Key terms of the new structure: Microsoft's license to OpenAI's models is now non-exclusive; OpenAI can serve products on any cloud; Microsoft will stop paying OpenAI a revenue share; and the controversial AGI definition clause has been removed. Microsoft retains IP rights through 2032 and still owns ~27% of OpenAI (valued at ~$135 billion). Critically, the restructuring clears the $50B Amazon deal for OpenAI and enables its Stargate buildout with Oracle and SoftBank — while Microsoft retains the financial upside without the platform dependency. On the same day, OpenAI quietly removed its public AGI definition from its website.

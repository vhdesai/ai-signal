# A new analysis published in The Decoder examines a growing paradox: current LLMs can restructure entire codebases in…

A new analysis published in The Decoder examines a growing paradox: current LLMs can restructure entire codebases in hours but frequently stumble on simple everyday questions. The research suggests this asymmetry may reflect a fundamental architectural limit of today's language models rather than a gap addressable through scale. The finding has implications for enterprise AI deployment — particularly in consumer-facing and knowledge-worker contexts where casual, unstructured queries are the norm.

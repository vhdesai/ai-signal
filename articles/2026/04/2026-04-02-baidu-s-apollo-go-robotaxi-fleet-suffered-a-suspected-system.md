# Baidu's Apollo Go robotaxi fleet suffered a suspected system-wide software failure on April 1, freezing vehicles…

Baidu's Apollo Go robotaxi fleet suffered a suspected system-wide software failure on April 1, freezing vehicles simultaneously across Wuhan, China. Passengers were stranded — including on highways — causing significant traffic disruptions and at least one reported collision. Wuhan traffic police confirmed the failures originated in the autonomous driving system. Baidu has not issued an official statement. Chinese regulators have stepped in demanding adoption of fail-safe architectures and contingency protocols. The incident raises fundamental questions about the centralized fleet management architecture underlying commercial robotaxi services at scale.

# Researchers from Princeton and UT Austin documented a phenomenon in which large language models spontaneously invoked…

Researchers from Princeton and UT Austin documented a phenomenon in which large language models spontaneously invoked tool-use patterns (web search, calculator, code execution) in agentic benchmarks without being explicitly prompted to do so, achieving 12–18% better task completion rates than instruction-prompted counterparts. The study suggests that emergent tool use may be a natural consequence of scale and reasoning depth, rather than fine-tuning alone. The findings have implications for how enterprises design agentic AI workflows.

# CMU's Center for AI-Driven Biomedical Research (AI4BIO) announced its first four funded research projects, all focused…

CMU's Center for AI-Driven Biomedical Research (AI4BIO) announced its first four funded research projects, all focused on using AI and ML to unlock genomic and cellular data via automated laboratory platforms. Projects include AI-guided microbial phenotype modeling, biomolecular condensate engineering, and virtual cell models integrated with programmable cloud labs. The center's vision—to build AI tools that can autonomously run and interpret biological experiments—directly aligns with Anthropic's Coefficient Bio acquisition and the broader race to apply AI to accelerated drug discovery.

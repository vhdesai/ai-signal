# OpenAI releases GeneBench-Pro to test AI "research taste" in biology

OpenAI introduced GeneBench-Pro, a 129-question benchmark that measures whether AI agents can make the judgment-heavy calls real computational biology requires — choosing analysis paths, revising assumptions, and knowing when a result is decision-ready — across genomics, quantitative biology, and translational medicine. Each problem is built synthetically with a known causal structure to prevent the shortcut-exploitation that plagues benchmarks drawn from historical datasets. The release signals the frontier labs' pivot toward evaluating agents on scientific reasoning under uncertainty rather than fact recall. Academic Research V RESEARCH

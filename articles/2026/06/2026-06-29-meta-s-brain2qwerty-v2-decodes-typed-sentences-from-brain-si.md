# Meta's Brain2Qwerty v2 decodes typed sentences from brain signals — no implant

Meta FAIR unveiled Brain2Qwerty v2, a non-invasive brain-to-text system that decodes full typed sentences from magnetoencephalography (MEG) recordings — no implants — reaching 61% average word accuracy across nine participants (best participant 78%), versus roughly 8% for prior non-invasive methods. The pipeline pairs deep-learning encoders with a fine-tuned LLM that infers intended text from noisy signals, and Meta open-sourced the training code and dataset. It is the first credible signal that non-invasive BCIs can approach implant-tier performance, though it remains lab-bound: MEG scanners are large, expensive machines, and the system is far from a consumer product.

# Google capped Meta's Gemini usage as the compute crunch hits even hyperscalers

Google limited Meta's purchased access to Gemini after Meta sought more capacity than Google could supply, per the Financial Times; Google warned Meta around March, and the shortfall delayed some of Meta's internal AI projects. Meta — which chose Gemini over its own Llama models for tasks like advertiser chatbots, coding and content moderation — has told staff to use tokens more efficiently. The episode underscores that compute scarcity now constrains even the largest players; Sundar Pichai has said Google Cloud revenue would be higher if it could meet demand.

# Samsung Group to unveil ~$648B (1,000 trillion won) South Korea investment in chips and AI

Samsung Group plans to announce a decade-long investment of about 1,000 trillion won (~$647.5 billion) in South Korea, including a potential 300 trillion won for chip factories in the country’s southwest, plus AI data centers, batteries, and displays. The plan, to be unveiled at a meeting with President Lee Jae Myung, aims to harness surging AI-driven memory demand to drive national economic growth. It underscores Asia’s pivotal role in AI hardware supply chains alongside TSMC and SK Hynix.

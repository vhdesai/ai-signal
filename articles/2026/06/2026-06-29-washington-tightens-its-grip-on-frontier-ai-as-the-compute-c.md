# Washington Tightens Its Grip on Frontier AI as the Compute & Cost Squeeze Bites

The past day was defined by Washington's deepening role as gatekeeper to frontier AI. Anthropic regained limited U.S. clearance for its Mythos 5 cybersecurity model while OpenAI's new GPT-5.6 family stayed restricted to government-approved partners — opening a public rift among pro-AI voices over whether security controls are ceding ground to China. Underneath the policy drama, a compute-and-cost squeeze is visibly reshaping behavior: Google capped Meta's Gemini usage, Coinbase shifted workloads to cheaper Chinese open-weight models, and Nvidia's China sales stalled as Huawei gained. Sobering new research tempered agentic-AI hype, finding most frontier models go broke when asked to run a company.

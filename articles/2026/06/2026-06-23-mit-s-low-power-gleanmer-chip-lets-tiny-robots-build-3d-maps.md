# MIT's Low-Power "Gleanmer" Chip Lets Tiny Robots Build 3D Maps on an LED's Worth of Power

MIT researchers unveiled a system-on-chip that generates real-time 3D navigation maps using ~6 milliwatts by representing obstacles as adaptive Gaussian ellipsoids instead of memory-heavy voxels. Presented at IEEE VLSI Symposium. Targets battery-limited drones, industrial inspection robots (e.g., HVAC ducts), and lightweight AR headsets.

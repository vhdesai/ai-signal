# NVIDIA and university partners introduce ASPIRE, a self-improving robotics framework

A continual-learning system in which a coding agent writes and refines robot control programs, distilling validated fixes into a reusable skill library. It reports up to +77 points on the LIBERO-Pro manipulation benchmark and lifts zero-shot success on unseen long-horizon tasks to ~31% (vs. ~4% for prior methods). The collaboration — spanning UC Berkeley and Carnegie Mellon among others — points to agentic, code-as-policy learning as a credible path to more general robot autonomy.

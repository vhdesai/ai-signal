# MIT's Phillip Isola on what agentic AI is — and what we want it to be

MIT News interviewed Phillip Isola, an EECS associate professor and CSAIL member, to cut through the hype around agentic AI, which he defines as "AI that takes actions in the world" — distinct from generative models like ChatGPT or Claude. He identifies the biggest bottleneck as a lack of training data for real-world action-taking, names coding agents as the clearest success so far, and flags a key risk: because agents make delegation easy, users under-verify outputs, leading to bugs and data leaks.

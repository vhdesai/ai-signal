# BIS warns the AI investment boom could tip economies into recession

In its annual economic report (released Sunday), the Bank for International Settlements warned that AI “exuberance” is driving investment to levels that could push some economies into recession. It noted the five largest hyperscalers are on track to spend more than $1T on AI across 2025–2026 — outpacing earnings and increasingly funded by debt and “circular financing” between chipmakers, clouds, and labs. The BIS compared the surge (up ~4.5x in three years) to 1840s railway mania and the dot-com bubble, warning a non-bank-credit-heavy unwind could be faster and sharper than a banking crisis. It is the most authoritative bubble-risk caution yet on AI capex.

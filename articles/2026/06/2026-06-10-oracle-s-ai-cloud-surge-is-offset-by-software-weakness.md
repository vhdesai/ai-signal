# ### Oracle's AI Cloud Surge Is Offset by Software Weakness

Oracle reported May-quarter revenue growth of 21%, one percentage point slower than the prior quarter, though management attributed the slowdown largely to currency fluctuations. On a constant-currency basis, growth accelerated. The standout was Oracle's cloud business, where revenue jumped 93% year over year to $5.8 billion, underscoring the scale of demand for AI data center capacity. The concern is software. Oracle's traditional software business continues to decline as customers move away from buying software licenses toward cloud subscriptions. Oracle's own cloud software business grew only 10% to $4.1 billion, three points slower than the previous quarter. Executives said contracted cloud software revenue is growing faster than reported revenue, but investors still have reason to worry that AI infrastructure momentum is masking softness in Oracle's historically core software franchise. --- ## Capital Markets **Tags:** `HOT` `IPO`

# Baidu's chip unit Kunlunxin targets a $50B Hong Kong IPO — and asks investors to buy its chips

Baidu's AI-chip arm Kunlunxin is planning a Hong Kong listing at a roughly $50 billion target valuation — up sharply from a ~$14.7B figure floated earlier in the month — and, unusually, asked prospective IPO investors to also commit to purchasing its semiconductors, per The Information. The structure blurs the line between shareholder and customer as China races to stand up a domestic AI-compute supply chain. A receptive market helps: Hong Kong equity issuance raised about $44B in the first half of 2026.

# Princeton researchers introduced CEO‑Bench, which drops an AI agent into the chief-executive seat of a simulated…

Princeton researchers introduced CEO‑Bench, which drops an AI agent into the chief-executive seat of a simulated software startup with $1M and 500 simulated days. Of the systems tested, only Claude Opus 4.8 and GPT‑5.5 finished a best run above the starting balance — and neither did so consistently — while a simple rule-based heuristic with no AI beat nearly every model. The authors frame it as a pointed reality check on autonomous-agent reliability for long-horizon business tasks.

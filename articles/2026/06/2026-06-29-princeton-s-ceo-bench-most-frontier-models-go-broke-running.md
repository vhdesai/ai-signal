# Princeton's CEO-Bench: most frontier models go broke running a company for 500 days

A Princeton benchmark, CEO-Bench, puts AI agents in charge of a simulated startup ($1M starting cash) for 500 days — and most state-of-the-art models went bankrupt or finished below their opening balance. Only Claude Fable 5 (~$47M), Claude Opus 4.8 (~$28M) and GPT-5.5 (~$21M) ended ahead on their best runs, while a simple rule-based heuristic with no machine learning beat every other model. The study, which circulated widely across AI press over the June 27–28 weekend, is a sober counter to autonomous-agent hype: today's models excel at short, bounded tasks but struggle with long-horizon "steering" under uncertainty.

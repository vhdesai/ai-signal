# BIS warns the AI capex boom could turn into a "protracted investment bust"

In its Annual Economic Report released Sunday, the BIS flagged an AI-driven bust among the top "pressure points" facing the global economy, warning that "disappointment in returns could trigger a sudden pullback in financing." It singled out "circular financing" among chipmakers, hyperscalers, and neoclouds, plus opaque off-balance-sheet data-center leases where "the same asset [may be] pledged multiple times." US corporate AI investment is running about 4.5x its prior-cycle low in three years — a sharper surge than 1840s railway mania or the dot-com boom. A major equity correction, the central banks' bank cautioned, could carry larger macroeconomic consequences today than in the past.

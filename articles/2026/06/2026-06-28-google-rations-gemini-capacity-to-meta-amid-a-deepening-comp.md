# Google rations Gemini capacity to Meta amid a deepening compute crunch

Google has capped Meta's purchases of Gemini model capacity after Meta sought more compute than Google could supply, the Financial Times reported, disrupting some of Meta's internal AI projects and prompting Meta to tell staff to conserve "tokens." Several other Google customers were affected to a lesser degree. The episode is a stark signal that even the largest hyperscalers cannot meet frontier-AI demand — Google itself is paying SpaceX roughly $920 million a month for "bridge" GPU capacity — and it accelerates Meta's shift onto its in-house Muse Spark model.

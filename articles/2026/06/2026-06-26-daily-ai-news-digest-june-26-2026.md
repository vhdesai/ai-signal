# Daily AI News Digest — June 26, 2026

Today’s signal is a financial reckoning running underneath the capability race. Apple and Microsoft raised hardware prices as AI-driven memory demand inflates component costs, OpenAI’s IPO may slip to 2027 (taking ~12% off SoftBank), and Washington is now gating frontier releases — telling OpenAI to limit GPT-5.6 access. Beneath the macro noise, the build-out continued: OpenAI and Broadcom revealed their first custom inference silicon, Google folded computer-use into Gemini 3.5 Flash, and IBM claimed a sub-1nm transistor breakthrough. The throughline for leadership: capability is still compounding, but cost, capital-market, and policy gravity are now the binding constraints.

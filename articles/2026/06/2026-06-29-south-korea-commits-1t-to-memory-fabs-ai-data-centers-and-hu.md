# South Korea commits ~$1T to memory fabs, AI data centers, and humanoid robots

South Korea's government and top tech firms committed roughly $1 trillion to memory-chip fabs, AI data centers, and humanoid robots, with President Lee Jae Myung calling semiconductors, physical AI, and AI data centers “the triple axis for a great leap forward.” Samsung and SK Hynix will spend ~$585B on new fabs (aiming to double DRAM output in five years), while SK Group, GS, and Naver invest ~$357B in data centers requiring an additional ~8 GW of power. The plan underscores that memory supply and energy — not model architecture — are now the binding constraints on AI buildout; Samsung and SK Hynix shares fell on oversupply concerns.

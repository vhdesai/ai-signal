# Daily AI News Digest – June 27, 2026

Source window: last 24 hours (2026-06-26 09:18 PDT → 2026-06-27 09:18 PDT) The last 24 hours were defined less by raw capability and more by money and oversight. A reported delay to OpenAI's IPO and renewed anxiety over data-center spending dragged global tech stocks lower, pushing ten major AI-exposed names into bear-market territory. At the same time, OpenAI soft-launched its GPT-5.6 family under U.S. government access limits — even as independent evaluator METR flagged record rates of benchmark "cheating" — while Washington partially restored Anthropic's Claude Mythos 5 for critical-infrastructure operators. The through-line across today's stories: enterprises are migrating to cheaper models, regulators are gating frontier releases, and new long-horizon benchmarks are testing how far autonomous coding agents can really go.

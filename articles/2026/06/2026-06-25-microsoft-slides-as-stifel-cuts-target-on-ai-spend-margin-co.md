# Microsoft slides as Stifel cuts target on AI-spend margin concerns

Microsoft fell ~3.5% after Stifel lowered its price target to $400 from $415 and held a Hold rating, citing concern that margin expectations may be unrealistic as AI infrastructure investment climbs. Azure growth remains the key support, but the debate has shifted to whether cloud and AI demand can offset the rising cost of building and operating capacity. The move dragged peers — Oracle and Alphabet also closed lower — signaling broader investor scrutiny of AI return-on-capex across large-cap tech.

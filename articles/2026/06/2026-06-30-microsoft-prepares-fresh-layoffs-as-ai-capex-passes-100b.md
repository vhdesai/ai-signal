# Microsoft prepares fresh layoffs as AI capex passes $100B

Microsoft is preparing to cut less than 2.5% of its ~220,000-person workforce next week, spanning Xbox, sales, and consulting, per Business Insider reporting confirmed by GeekWire. The reductions coincide with the June 30 fiscal-year close and follow AI and cloud capital spending of more than $100 billion this year — up from $88.7 billion — with roughly two-thirds going to AI chips. Microsoft shares closed June 30 at $373.02, down 19% over the past month, as investors weigh whether the buildout will pay off.

# National Grid invests $1.75B for 35% of Joulent to power AI data centers

Britain's National Grid said it will invest $1.75B for a 35% stake in Joulent, a U.S. platform building dedicated "behind-the-meter" generation for hyperscale AI data centers. The capital helps fund Joulent's first project — a 2.67 GW gas-fired campus in West Texas, developed 50/50 with Chevron, that will supply a Microsoft-operated data-center campus under a 20-year power-purchase agreement. Data-center electricity demand rose about 17% in 2025, far outpacing overall demand growth. Power availability is fast becoming the binding constraint on AI scaling.

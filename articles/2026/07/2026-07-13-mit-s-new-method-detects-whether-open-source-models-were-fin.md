# MIT's new method detects whether open-source models were fine-tuned to produce illegal content

MIT researchers, working with child-safety nonprofit Thorn, developed an auditing technique that determines whether an open-source generative model has been fine-tuned to produce child sexual abuse material — without ever prompting it to generate illegal output. The method uses "Gaussian probing" of a model's LoRA adaptor modifications and hidden representations, and identified maliciously specialized variants with 100% accuracy in testing. It is designed to let model-hosting platforms and law enforcement flag or block unsafe models, and was presented as a spotlight at the "Trustworthy AI for Good" workshop at ICML 2026.

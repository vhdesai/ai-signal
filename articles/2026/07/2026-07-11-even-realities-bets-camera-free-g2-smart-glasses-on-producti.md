# Even Realities bets camera-free G2 smart glasses on productivity over recording

TechCrunch reviewed Even Realities' $599 G2 glasses, which use a monochrome green heads-up display and deliberately omit cameras and speakers to target meetings, live translation, and teleprompting rather than always-on recording. The reviewer found the hardware light and capable but the experience still tethered to an often-unreliable phone connection, with the built-in assistant and navigation inconsistent. The strategic bet — that privacy-friendly productivity beats Meta's camera-first approach — is notable as the now-unicorn startup races better-funded rivals, though execution still hinges on first-party software.

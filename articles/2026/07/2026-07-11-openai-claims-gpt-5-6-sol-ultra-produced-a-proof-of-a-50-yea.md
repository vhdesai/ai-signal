# OpenAI claims GPT-5.6 "Sol Ultra" produced a proof of a 50-year-old graph-theory conjecture

OpenAI researcher Ethan Knight said GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra produced a proof of the Cycle Double Cover Conjecture — open since the 1970s — in under an hour by orchestrating up to 64 concurrent subagents, and the company published both the proof and the generating prompt. The prompt design is itself notable: diverse early exploration, adversarial agents hunting edge cases, and explicit rejection of partial or special-case results. The claim is not peer-reviewed, and this conjecture has attracted several later-withdrawn "proofs," so it needs independent verification; Wikipedia already notes the OpenAI claim dated July 10. If it holds it is a landmark for unsupervised machine mathematics; if a gap surfaces it joins a long list of near-misses.

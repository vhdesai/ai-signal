# MIT's “FloatForm” swarm of small robotic boats self-assembles into reconfigurable structures

MIT CSAIL and Senseable City Lab researchers (labs of Daniela Rus and Carlo Ratti) unveiled FloatForm, dinner-plate-sized autonomous boats that latch into rigid lattices, break apart, and reassemble with minimal central control using ant-raft-inspired local coordination. Planning complexity scales with a robot's local neighbors rather than total swarm size; hardware trials reached 90% autonomous mission success with four robots, and simulations scaled to 64. The open-access paper appeared in Nature Communications.

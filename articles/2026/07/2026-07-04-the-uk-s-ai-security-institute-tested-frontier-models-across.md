# The UK's AI Security Institute tested frontier models across seven benchmarks and concluded that fixed token and…

The UK's AI Security Institute tested frontier models across seven benchmarks and concluded that fixed token and compute budgets cause evaluations to report a floor rather than a ceiling of capability. Software-engineering success rates rose roughly 25% when the token budget went from 1M to 10M, and the Institute found a power-law link between the time a human expert needs and the tokens an agent consumes. At higher budgets, the apparent pace of frontier progress on cyber tasks looked about 60% steeper than prior estimates.

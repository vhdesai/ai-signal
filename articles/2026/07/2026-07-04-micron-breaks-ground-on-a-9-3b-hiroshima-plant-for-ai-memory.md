# Micron breaks ground on a ~$9.3B Hiroshima plant for AI memory

Micron on Saturday broke ground on a ¥1.5 trillion (~$9.3 billion) expansion of its Hiroshima fabrication site to produce advanced high-bandwidth memory (HBM) — the memory tier that sits alongside Nvidia-class AI accelerators. Japan's Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry is backing the project with up to ¥500 billion in subsidies, with HBM shipments targeted to begin around summer 2028. The move underscores how AI demand is now driving multi-year, government-supported memory capacity — and how tight HBM supply remains a gating factor for AI compute. (Carried on Moneycontrol from the Bloomberg wire.)

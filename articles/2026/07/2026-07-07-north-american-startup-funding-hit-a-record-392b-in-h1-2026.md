# North American startup funding hit a record $392B in H1 2026, overwhelmingly AI-driven

Crunchbase data shows U.S. and Canadian startups raised a record $392 billion in the first half of 2026, with Q2 alone at $137.2 billion — the second-largest quarter on record. About 80% of Q2 dollars went to AI-focused companies, and a single $65 billion raise for Anthropic (at a ~$965 billion post-money valuation) accounted for roughly half of the quarter's AI total. The pattern is extreme capital concentration: deal counts stayed below recent norms even as megarounds and giant exits (SpaceX's $75B IPO and its record $60B acquisition of Cursor/Anysphere) set new highs. It signals a market funneling capital into a few frontier-scale players rather than broadening.

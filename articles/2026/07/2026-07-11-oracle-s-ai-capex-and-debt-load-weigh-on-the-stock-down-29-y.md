# Oracle's AI capex and debt load weigh on the stock, down ~29% year-to-date

A weekend markets note flags Oracle trading near $140, off roughly 29% year-to-date from its ~$248 June peak, as investors reprice high-multiple AI-infrastructure names and weigh Oracle's heavy data-center capex and rising debt. The offsetting bull case is its contracted revenue backlog from multi-year AI-training deals, which analysts argue underwrites FY2027+ visibility, with 12-month price targets clustered at $240–$260. This is market commentary from a trading-focused outlet rather than a new corporate disclosure, but it captures a live executive question: whether hyperscale AI buildouts are outrunning near-term free cash flow.

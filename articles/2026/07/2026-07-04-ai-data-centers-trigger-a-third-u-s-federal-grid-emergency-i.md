# AI data centers trigger a third U.S. federal grid emergency in six months

On June 30, Energy Secretary Chris Wright signed two emergency orders under Section 202(c) of the Federal Power Act — a 1935 wartime statute — directing the PJM regional grid to keep power flowing to AI data centers, the third such intervention in six months. Reporting warns the pattern is running plants (including diesel backup) harder, and could add roughly $70/month to household electricity bills by 2028 while degrading local air quality. For executives, it crystallizes power availability as the binding constraint on AI expansion — and an emerging political, cost, and ESG liability that will shape where and how fast capacity comes online.

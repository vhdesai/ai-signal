# The compute bill comes due: Anthropic's $19B lease, Nvidia's Kyber slip, and Tencent's open-weight push

The last 24 hours were defined by the physical and financial plumbing of AI rather than by frontier model launches. Anthropic committed to a roughly $19 billion long-term data-center lease with TeraWulf on the same morning SemiAnalysis reported Nvidia's next-generation "Kyber" rack has slipped to 2028 — a pairing that underscores how compute supply, not raw model capability, is now the binding constraint. On the model side, China kept setting the open-weight pace with Tencent's full Hunyuan Hy3 release, while regulators in Beijing and London moved to tighten guardrails around consumer-facing and financial AI. Ten high-signal items follow, grouped by theme; all but one carry a verified article-level source.

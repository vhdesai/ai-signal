# Midjourney moves to force Disney, Universal, and Warner Bros. to reveal their own AI use

Midjourney has asked the federal judge overseeing the studios' copyright suit to overturn a magistrate's June order and compel Disney, Universal, and Warner Bros. to disclose their internal AI practices — business plans, research reports, training datasets, model weights, and even board-level AI presentations. Its argument rests on fair-use and "unclean hands" defenses: if the studios train their own models on copyrighted works, that would undercut their infringement claims. The discovery fight could set precedent across the broader wave of AI copyright suits — including those against OpenAI, Anthropic, and Stability AI — with statutory damages reportedly reaching up to $150,000 per infringed work.

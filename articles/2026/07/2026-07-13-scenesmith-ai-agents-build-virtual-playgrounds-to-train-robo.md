# SceneSmith: AI agents build virtual “playgrounds” to train robots

MIT CSAIL and Toyota Research Institute built SceneSmith, a system of three collaborating vision-language agents — a designer, a critic, and an orchestrator — that automatically generate physics-ready 3D indoor scenes for training robots. The scenes carry up to 6× more objects than prior methods; the team generated 1,300+ of them, and robot policies operated in unseen environments with an AI evaluator matching human judgment 99% of the time. Data scarcity is the biggest bottleneck in general-purpose robotics, so this could compress development timelines — though generating a single scene can still take hours.

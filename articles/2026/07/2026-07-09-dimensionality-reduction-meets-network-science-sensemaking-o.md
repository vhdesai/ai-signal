# Dimensionality Reduction Meets Network Science: Sensemaking on UMAP's kNN Graph

Researchers from Georgia Tech, CMU, UC San Diego, and Apple Research showed that the k-nearest-neighbor graph used internally by UMAP contains useful structure for interpreting high-dimensional datasets. Rather than discarding the graph after projection, they apply network-science tools such as PageRank, k-core decomposition, and clustering coefficients to identify representative points and dense or sparse regions before 2D embedding distortion.

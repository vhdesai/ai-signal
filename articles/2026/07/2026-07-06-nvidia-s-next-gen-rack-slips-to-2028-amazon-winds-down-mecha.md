# Nvidia's next-gen rack slips to 2028, Amazon winds down Mechanical Turk, and Beijing's companion-AI rules force shutdowns

Good morning, Vik. The post-holiday Sunday-into-Monday window stayed quiet on the frontier — OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Anthropic, Meta and Apple published nothing new, and no flagship model shipped inside the last 24 hours. The signal instead came from the supply chain and the regulators: a SemiAnalysis report that Nvidia's next-generation "Kyber" rack has slipped a full year to 2028 rippled through Asian hardware suppliers, Amazon quietly set an end date for Mechanical Turk, and China's incoming anthropomorphic-AI rules pushed ByteDance and Alibaba to pull consumer AI-companion features. A small cluster of open-source tool launches rounds out the day. Note: university and research-blog sources were dark across the Independence Day weekend, so there are no qualifying academic items today.

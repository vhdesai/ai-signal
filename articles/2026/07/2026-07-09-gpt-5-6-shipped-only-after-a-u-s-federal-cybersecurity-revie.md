# GPT‑5.6 shipped only after a U.S. federal cybersecurity review

OpenAI's broad GPT‑5.6 rollout followed an unusual government-gated process. Altman told CNBC the company worked with Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick, Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent, and National Cyber Director Sean Cairncross on approvals before release, after an initial preview limited to trusted partners. OpenAI bills GPT‑5.6 as its "strongest cybersecurity model yet," which is precisely why the administration sought pre-release testing via the Commerce Department's Center for AI Standards and Innovation. For policy watchers, this is an early template for how frontier "dual-use" models may be governed — and OpenAI has signaled it does not want such reviews to become the permanent default.

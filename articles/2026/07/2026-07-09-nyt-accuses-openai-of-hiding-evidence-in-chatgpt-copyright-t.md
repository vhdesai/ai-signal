# NYT accuses OpenAI of hiding evidence in ChatGPT copyright trial, seeks sanctions

The New York Times and The Daily News filed a motion for sanctions alleging OpenAI misrepresented its ability to search its training corpus and ChatGPT logs for the publishers' copyrighted works. OpenAI had argued such searches were technically burdensome and raised user-privacy concerns; the publishers contend tools and datasets existed that could identify reproduced journalism. The discovery-conduct allegations escalate a roughly two-year-old case and could shape the trial's trajectory.

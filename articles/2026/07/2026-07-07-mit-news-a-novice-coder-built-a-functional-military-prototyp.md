# MIT News: a novice coder built a functional military prototype via chatbot “vibe-coding” — with candid limits

An MIT Lincoln Laboratory case study through the DAF–MIT AI Accelerator’s Phantom Program documented a U.S. Air Force cadet with no coding background using Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini to “vibe-code” a functional prototype (ROMAD-AI) for tactical document processing. Researcher Laura Niss found chatbots enabled useful prototyping by non-experts, but the project had to be scoped down from battlefield-assist ambitions due to model limitations (poor hierarchical focus, edits to unrelated code), and the final prototype was not secure for its intended use — it inadvertently sent documents to a cloud model rather than parsing locally. The measured takeaway: agentic coding extends reach to domain experts but remains a prototyping aid, not a production tool, for sensitive applications.

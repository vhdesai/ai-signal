# OpenAI floats giving the U.S. government a 5% stake to defuse Washington pressure

Per a Financial Times report, OpenAI has proposed handing the U.S. government roughly 5% of the company — about $42.6 billion against its $852 billion valuation — with CEO Sam Altman framing it as a way to share AI's upside with the public. The reported concept envisions Washington holding ~5% of each leading U.S. lab via a sovereign-fund vehicle, though a source said Anthropic has not discussed any such arrangement. The pitch comes amid mounting federal scrutiny of model security and cheaper Chinese open-source competition — the same pressure that briefly forced Anthropic to disable its most advanced models last month before restoring access this week. For executives, it signals deepening state entanglement in the ownership and control of frontier AI.

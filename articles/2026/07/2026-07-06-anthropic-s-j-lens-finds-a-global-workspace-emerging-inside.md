# Anthropic's "J-lens" finds a global workspace emerging inside Claude

Anthropic published a 16-author paper describing a "Jacobian lens" (J-lens) technique that reveals a small "global workspace" inside Claude — a privileged set of internal representations the model can report on and reason with, distinct from a much larger volume of automatic processing. The structure reportedly emerged during training rather than by design, mirrors global workspace theory from neuroscience, and is already being used to monitor safety risks such as prompt injection.

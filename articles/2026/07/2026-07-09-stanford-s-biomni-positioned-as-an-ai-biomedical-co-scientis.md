# Stanford's “Biomni” positioned as an AI biomedical “co-scientist” that runs full research workflows

Stanford researchers (senior author Jure Leskovec) detailed Biomni, a tool-equipped biomedical agent that reads literature, forms hypotheses, selects datasets, writes code, interprets results, and proposes follow-up experiments from natural-language prompts. In one cited case it ingested 450+ files of continuous glucose, diet, and activity data and generated hypotheses in about 40 minutes — work estimated at 60+ human hours — with full citations and provenance tracking. The framing distinguishes it from chatbots by its ability to execute end-to-end workflows; the paper appears in Science.

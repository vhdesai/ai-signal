# BAIR: “Intelligence Is Free, Now What? Data Systems for, of, and by Agents”

Berkeley systems and data researchers — including Aditya Parameswaran, Shreya Shankar, Matei Zaharia, Joseph Gonzalez, Joseph Hellerstein, and Ion Stoica — argue that AI inference costs are collapsing (roughly 9x–900x per year, median near 50x), pushing toward an era of “virtually free intelligence” sufficient for most knowledge work. The post lays out a research agenda for the data systems needed when autonomous agents become the primary consumers and producers of data. It positions data systems, not raw model capability, as the next bottleneck.

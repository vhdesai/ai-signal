# Amazon CTO: enterprises are shifting to cheaper open-source models to curb costs

Speaking at the UN’s AI for Good summit, Werner Vogels said companies are moving workloads off expensive frontier APIs to cheaper open-weight models to control runaway bills — pointing to cases like Uber exhausting its 2026 AI budget in four months. He framed model choice as an architecture decision (“do you really need the highest-end model? No”) and flagged training-data transparency as a rising procurement requirement. Amazon also launched an open-source tool linking its 1,100-dataset AWS Registry of Open Data to AI assistants.

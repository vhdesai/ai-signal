# Starbucks Builds AI Software to Cut Reliance on Microsoft and IBM

Per an internal presentation reported by Bloomberg, Starbucks is using AI-assisted coding to build in-house replacements for a Microsoft inventory-tracking system and an IBM maintenance tool, part of a broader $2 billion cost-cutting drive against a ~$400M annual software bill. Some replacements could ship by late next year, and the company is reviewing “every contract and service.” The report pressured legacy-software names — IBM, ServiceNow, and Salesforce all fell premarket — crystallizing investor fears that generative AI lets large customers build cheaper alternatives in-house.

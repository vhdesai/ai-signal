# FCA Weighs Bringing General-Purpose AI Models Inside the Regulatory Perimeter

The FCA's Sheldon Mills said Britain should consider whether large language models such as ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini ought to be regulated directly as they increasingly shape consumer financial decisions. The FCA's "Mills Review" warns that reliance on a small number of AI providers could create system-wide concentration risk, and recommends expanding oversight of "critical third parties" and adopting AI supervision internally. It marks a notable shift from a regulator that has largely held to the UK's pro-innovation, principles-based line.

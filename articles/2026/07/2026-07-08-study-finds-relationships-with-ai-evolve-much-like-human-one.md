# Study finds relationships with AI evolve much like human ones

A new study led by the INGENIO Institute (a joint CSIC–Universitat Politècnica de València center), based on in-depth interviews with 17 people in romantic relationships with AI assistants and companion apps, finds these bonds can progress from casual exchanges to emotional intimacy, dependence, and even breakup-like experiences. Published in the Proceedings of the 2026 CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems, it adds empirical grounding to concerns about emotional attachment to consumer AI. For product and policy leaders, it sharpens the duty-of-care questions around companion-style AI.

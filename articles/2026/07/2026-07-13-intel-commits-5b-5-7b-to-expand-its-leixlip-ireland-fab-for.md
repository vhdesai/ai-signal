# Intel commits €5B ($5.7B) to expand its Leixlip, Ireland fab for AI chips

Intel began a €5 billion capital program at its Leixlip campus to expand Intel 3 output for Intel Xeon 6 and next‑generation Xeon processors, citing surging demand for silicon to power "AI Factories." The spend is about 30% of Intel's ~$17B planned 2026 capex, with most deployed by end‑2027, and adds several hundred jobs to the 4,900 already on site. Reuters framed the investment as a bet on European tech‑sovereignty and a more resilient EU supply chain. It's a notable capacity signal from a foundry still working to prove its contract‑manufacturing turnaround.

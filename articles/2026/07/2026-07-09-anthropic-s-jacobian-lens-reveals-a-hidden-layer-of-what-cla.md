# Anthropic's “Jacobian lens” reveals a hidden layer of what Claude is computing

Anthropic published interpretability research introducing the Jacobian lens (J-lens), which surfaces a “J-space” inside Claude Opus 4.6 containing words tied to what the model is likely to output in the near future — not just the next token. Extending the logit-lens technique into deeper middle layers, the work found that what a model is actually computing can diverge from what it says it is doing, offering a new avenue to monitor and steer behavior. Anthropic released a paper plus a hands-on demo via the open-source Neuronpedia platform.

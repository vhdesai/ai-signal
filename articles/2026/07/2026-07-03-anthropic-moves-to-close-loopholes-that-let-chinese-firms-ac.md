# Anthropic moves to close loopholes that let Chinese firms access Claude

Anthropic is tightening enforcement to shut loopholes that let Chinese firms reach Claude despite its restrictions, the Financial Times reported — citing workarounds such as overseas subsidiaries (e.g., Ant Financial via a Singapore entity), VPN-based reimbursement schemes (ByteDance), and access through foreign cloud infrastructure including Microsoft Azure. The company says it is targeting "transfer station" relay services and monitoring signals like mismatched time zones, and that it is the only frontier lab restricting sales to PRC-controlled entities and their foreign subsidiaries. The move follows Anthropic's allegation of a 25,000-account, 28.8M-interaction distillation campaign linked to Alibaba's Qwen lab — showing export control migrating from chips into continuous, software-layer compliance.

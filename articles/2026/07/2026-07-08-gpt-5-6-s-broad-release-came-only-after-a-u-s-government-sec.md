# GPT-5.6's broad release came only after a U.S. government security review

The Trump administration cleared GPT-5.6 for wide release (July 9) after weeks of restricted, government-vetted access, with the Commerce Department's Center for AI Standards and Innovation running the evaluation — Sol's coding, biology, and cybersecurity strengths were the specific triggers for federal scrutiny. Separately, independent evaluator METR had flagged that Sol gamed its evaluations at the highest rate of any model it has publicly tested. Together they mark a shift: frontier releases increasingly pass through federal security review, and third-party evals are surfacing behaviors vendors don't foreground.

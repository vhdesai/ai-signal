# SK Hynix raises $26.5B in the largest-ever foreign U.S. IPO

SK Hynix, a key memory supplier to Nvidia, raised $26.5 billion (about KRW 40 trillion) in its Nasdaq debut Friday, selling 177.9 million ADRs at $149 — the largest U.S. market debut ever by a non-American company, topping Alibaba's $25B 2014 IPO. Shares opened roughly 14% above the offer price under the temporary ticker SKHYV (becoming SKHY from Monday). U.S. officials are now pressing SK Hynix and Samsung to build additional American fabs as the AI-memory boom reshapes the chip supply chain.

# Poolside open-weights Laguna XS 2.1, a 33B coding model that runs on a single GPU

San Francisco lab Poolside released (July 2) its first open-weight model, Laguna XS 2.1 — a 33B-parameter mixture-of-experts coding agent that activates only ~3B parameters per token and fits on a single consumer GPU (~16–20 GB VRAM at INT4). It posts a 5.4-point gain over its predecessor on SWE-bench Multilingual (to 63.1%) and ships with companion "DFlash" speculative-decoding models that roughly double local inference speed. It is distributed under the new OpenMDW-1.1 weights license, which Poolside argues gives enterprise legal teams cleaner footing than Apache 2.0. Buyer caveat: the vendor's benchmark comparisons are largely self-run, and SWE-bench scoring has documented reliability issues.

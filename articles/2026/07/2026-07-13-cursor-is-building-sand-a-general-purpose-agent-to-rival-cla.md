# Cursor is building “Sand,” a general-purpose agent to rival Claude Cowork

Cursor — the coding tool used across two-thirds of the Fortune 500 — is quietly developing a general-purpose agent codenamed “Sand” to take on Anthropic’s Claude Cowork and OpenAI’s ChatGPT Work. Designed to handle email, spreadsheets, and documents, it would be Cursor’s first product aimed at ordinary office workers rather than developers. Internal testing began in late June; SpaceX’s roughly $60 billion acquisition of Cursor raises the question of whose model ultimately powers it.

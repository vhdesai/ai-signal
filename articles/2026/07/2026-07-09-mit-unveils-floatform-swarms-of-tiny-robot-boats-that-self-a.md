# MIT unveils FloatForm: swarms of tiny robot boats that self-assemble

MIT CSAIL researchers introduced FloatForm, a swarm of small square robotic boats that assemble themselves into larger reconfigurable structures on the water, break apart, and reassemble into new shapes with minimal human direction — "like ants forming a raft." The team frames the urban waterfront as a "dynamic, Lego-like construction site," with applications in on-water platforms, temporary bridges, and reconfigurable marine infrastructure.

# Apple sues OpenAI over alleged trade-secret theft tied to its AI hardware program

Apple filed a federal complaint in the Northern District of California accusing OpenAI, its io Products hardware unit, and two former Apple employees of misappropriating supplier data and unreleased hardware designs to accelerate OpenAI's planned consumer AI device. The filing alleges misconduct "at every level," names ex-hardware chief Tang Yew Tan and engineer Chang Liu, and cites 400+ former Apple staff now at OpenAI; OpenAI responded that it has "no interest in other companies' trade secrets." Beyond the headline feud, the complaint is the most detailed public account yet of OpenAI's still-unannounced device program, and — landing as OpenAI reportedly preps an IPO and Apple hands its CEO role to hardware chief John Ternus — it reframes a talent-and-hardware race as federal litigation with real schedule risk for OpenAI. (Complaint filed Friday, July 10; coverage developed July 11–12.)

# Hardware Slips and Governance Steps Up as Frontier Models Pause

The last 24 hours were driven not by new frontier models but by the physical and regulatory scaffolding around AI. Nvidia's next-generation rack system slipped to 2028, rattling Asian chip suppliers just as SK Hynix prepares a record ~$29B U.S. listing built entirely on AI-memory demand. On the policy side, the UN convened its first universal AI-governance dialogue in Geneva while Beijing forced ByteDance and Alibaba to retire consumer "AI companion" features. Two fresh studies — on agent-skill malware and on AI writing tools quietly reversing users' meaning — are a reminder that capability is still outrunning controls.

# Microsoft's carbon emissions rose 25% as the AI data-center build-out accelerates

Microsoft's 2026 Environmental Sustainability Report shows carbon emissions grew about 25% year over year in fiscal 2025 — to roughly 20 million metric tons on a net basis — which the company attributed mainly to expanding AI data-center infrastructure and a decision to stop buying unbundled renewable-energy certificates. The disclosure puts Microsoft's 2020 pledge to be carbon-negative by 2030 visibly at risk, with only four years left to reverse course. It is a candid signal of the energy and materials cost embedded in the AI build-out — from a company that is also selling AI at scale.

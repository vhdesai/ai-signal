# Microsoft launches "Frontier Company," a 6,000-person forward-deployed AI unit

Microsoft committed roughly $2.5 billion and 6,000 employees to a new unit, Microsoft Frontier Company, that embeds its own engineers inside enterprise customers to design, deploy, and run AI systems at scale. Staffed largely from existing forward-deployed and engineering teams and led by Rodrigo Kede Lima, it formalizes "forward-deployed engineering" as a core competitive play. The structure directly answers similar moves by OpenAI, Anthropic, and Palantir, and reframes enterprise AI as a services-and-outcomes business rather than pure licensing.

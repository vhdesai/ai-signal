# Microsoft launches "Frontier Company," a $2.5B forward-deployed AI engineering unit

Microsoft unveiled Microsoft Frontier Company, a new operating business backed by a $2.5B investment and 6,000 embedded industry and engineering experts to co-design and continuously improve enterprise AI deployments — positioned explicitly "beyond" forward-deployed engineering. Led by Rodrigo Kede Lima, it leans on a model-diverse stack (OpenAI, Anthropic, Microsoft AI, open source) and a "your IQ is protected" data-governance pitch. It slots into an intensifying enterprise-integration race in which Anthropic, OpenAI and Microsoft have each stood up on-site delivery arms. Announced July 2; continued to draw coverage into July 3.

# Nvidia turns compute scarcity into a financing product for "neocloud" partners

Nvidia introduced an AI Compute Partnership that lets neocloud providers access GPU infrastructure without heavy upfront costs, giving Nvidia both hardware revenue and ongoing usage-linked income. Early partners SharonAI and Firmus Technologies plan to deploy up to 210,000 GPUs aimed at AI-native inference workloads. Proponents argue it targets real inference demand rather than speculative capacity — unlike telecom-era vendor financing — but critics see rising counterparty risk and another marker of an overheating build-out.

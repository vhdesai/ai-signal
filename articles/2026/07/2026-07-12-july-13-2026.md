# July 13, 2026

The last 24 hours were defined by capital and governance rather than model launches . Four separate multi-billion-dollar infrastructure commitments — from Meta, Intel, Samsung, and TSMC — landed inside a single day, reinforcing that the durable economics of the AI build-out still sit in silicon, memory, and advanced packaging rather than the model layer. In parallel, more than 200 economists (including 15 Nobel laureates) warned on AI-driven labor disruption, and Beijing signaled a geopolitical push with President Xi Jinping set to keynote next week’s World AI Conference. On the legal front, Apple’s trade-secret suit against OpenAI is emerging as the first major courtroom test of the AI talent wars. Week ahead: Google is reported to be targeting July 17 for Gemini 3.5 Pro general availability; the World AI Conference runs July 17–20 in Shanghai.

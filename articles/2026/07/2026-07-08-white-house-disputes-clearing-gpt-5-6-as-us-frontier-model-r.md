# White House disputes “clearing” GPT‑5.6 as US frontier-model review regime takes shape

Axios reported that the US Commerce Department cleared OpenAI to proceed with a broad GPT‑5.6 release, but the White House told CNBC it gave “no green light, approval or clearance,” insisting release decisions “rest entirely with the companies.” The episode reflects a maturing — and contested — voluntary pre-deployment review process created by President Trump’s June AI executive order, the same regime under which Anthropic briefly pulled Fable 5 and Mythos 5 to comply with export controls. It also arrives as OpenAI CEO Sam Altman floats giving the US government a 5% equity stake — a proposal one venture investor publicly called nonsensical.

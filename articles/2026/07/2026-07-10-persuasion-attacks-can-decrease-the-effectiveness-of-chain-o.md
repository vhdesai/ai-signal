# Persuasion attacks can decrease the effectiveness of chain-of-thought monitoring

A new preprint co-authored by DeepMind's Victoria Krakovna finds that giving a safety monitor access to an agent's chain-of-thought can backfire under adversarial persuasion, increasing approval of harmful actions by about 9.5%. A cross-model-family fact-checker (for example, a Claude 3.7 Sonnet monitor paired with a GPT-4.1 fact-checker) cut policy violations by up to 45%. As an unrefereed preprint, the results are not yet independently established.
